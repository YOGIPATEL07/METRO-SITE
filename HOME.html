POLAROID 320

The Polaroid Automatic 320 Camera was a popular instant camera model that was first introduced in 1969. It was part of the Polaroid Land Camera series and used the peel-apart instant film. The camera featured a folding rangefinder design and a built-in automatic exposure system, which made it easy for users to take pictures without having to manually adjust the settings

POLAROID SX-70

The Polaroid SX-70 was a revolutionary instant camera that was first introduced in 1972. It was designed by Edwin Land and was the first instant camera to use Polaroid's integral instant film, which contained both the negative and the positive layers required for a finished photograph.

POLAROID PRONTO B

The Polaroid Pronto B was an instant camera that was introduced in the 1970s. It used Polaroid's SX-70 film and featured a folding design similar to the SX-70. The camera was notable for its compact size and its built-in automatic flash.
